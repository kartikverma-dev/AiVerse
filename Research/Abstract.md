# Research Abstract

This paper presents the conceptual architecture and empirical evaluation of **CredgeAiVerse**, an industrial-grade knowledge evolution and status forecasting engine for artificial intelligence paradigms. The explosive growth of AI research and tooling has introduced significant cognitive overload and semantic volatility, rendering traditional static documentation paradigms obsolete. To address this, we propose an automated pipeline that tracks the birth, growth, stability, decline, and historical migration of technical concepts. By extracting lineage trees from citations and measuring popularity using real-time repository stars, citation frequency, and social volumes, our platform constructs a dynamic knowledge graph. Experimental verification confirms that the automated pipeline successfully classifies concept status with high alignment to consensus evaluations, providing a scalable model for automated computer science ontology curation.
